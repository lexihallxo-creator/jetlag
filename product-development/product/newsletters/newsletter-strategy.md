# JetLag Newsletter Strategy

## Goal

Create an ultra-distinctive newsletter that makes JetLag the most useful voice in travel recovery and time-zone performance.

## Why This Matters

- It gives JetLag a trust-building channel before the full product matures
- It creates repeat attention instead of one-off traffic
- It becomes a conversion path into concierge plans, subscriptions, and future products

## Positioning

This should not feel like a generic travel newsletter.

It should feel like a field guide for people who need to function across time zones.

## Editorial Promise

Every issue should help the reader do one of the following:

- recover faster from an upcoming trip
- avoid a common travel-performance mistake
- understand why a tactic works
- feel smarter about time-zone adaptation than the average traveler

## What Makes It Unique

- It focuses on travel performance, not broad wellness fluff
- It translates science into practical decisions
- It uses real-world scenarios, not abstract advice
- It can combine concierge insight, founder observation, and live market learning from daily scans

## Voice

- Calm
- Sharp
- Practical
- Slightly contrarian when useful
- Never preachy

## Audience

- Frequent business travelers
- International leisure travelers who care about energy and trip quality
- Digital nomads
- Performance-minded travelers who like intelligent systems more than lifestyle fluff

## Newsletter Jobs To Be Done

- Make JetLag memorable
- Build authority before asking for a sale
- Give people a reason to forward the content
- Surface pain points and objections for the product roadmap

## Conversion Paths

- CTA to JetLag Express concierge plan
- CTA to waitlist or product signup
- CTA to reply with upcoming trip details
- CTA to forward the issue to a traveler flying soon
