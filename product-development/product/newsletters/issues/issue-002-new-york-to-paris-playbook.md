# Issue 002

## Subject Line

The New York to Paris Monday meeting playbook

## Opening

The hardest international trip is not the longest one.

It is the one where you need to sound intelligent less than 24 hours after landing.

New York to Paris is a perfect example. It is short enough to underestimate and brutal enough to expose every lazy jet lag habit you have.

## Why This Happens

Most travelers treat this route like a normal overnight flight.

They stay up too long before departure, sleep randomly on the plane, land in a haze, and then improvise the first day in Paris. That creates a second crash right when they need focus.

## The Playbook

Here is the better way to think about it:

1. Protect the Paris bedtime you want before you leave New York.
2. Use the plane for strategic rest, not emotional sleep.
3. Treat arrival day like schedule-setting time, not dead time.

## What To Do Instead

- Go into the flight with a clear idea of whether sleep on the plane helps or hurts your first night in Paris.
- Keep caffeine intentional instead of using it all day out of panic.
- Get useful light exposure after arrival.
- Avoid the late nap that feels harmless but destroys the next sleep window.

## Scenario

You land Sunday morning and have a Monday morning meeting.

The question is not, "How do I feel okay right now?"

The question is, "What gives me the best odds of sleeping properly Sunday night and being coherent Monday morning?"

That framing changes everything.

## Close

Good jet lag strategy is not about surviving the flight. It is about winning the first important moment after the flight.
