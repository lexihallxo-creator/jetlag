# Issue 003

## Subject Line

The biggest lie in jet lag advice: just sleep on the plane

## Opening

"Just sleep on the plane" might be the most repeated bad advice in travel recovery.

It sounds smart because sleep feels like the obvious fix.

But sleep that happens at the wrong time can make the next 24 hours worse, not better.

## Why This Advice Fails

Plane sleep is not automatically useful sleep.

What matters is whether that sleep helps you land closer to the destination rhythm or keeps you glued to the clock you are trying to leave behind.

## The Real Question

Do you need that in-flight sleep to support the next local bedtime, or will it make it harder to sleep when the destination actually needs you to?

That is the question most generic advice skips.

## What To Do Instead

- Decide in advance whether the plane is for rest, light sleep, or staying awake
- Think about the next important sleep anchor, not just immediate fatigue
- Use caffeine, light, and arrival timing as part of one system

## Scenario

Two travelers take the same route.

One sleeps impulsively because they are tired. The other uses the flight to support a destination-night strategy.

They can land on the same runway and still have completely different outcomes the next day.

## Close

The goal is not to sleep whenever possible. The goal is to sleep when it helps the destination clock win.
