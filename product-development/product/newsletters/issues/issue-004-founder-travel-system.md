# Issue 004

## Subject Line

The founder travel system: how to stay useful across time zones

## Opening

Founders do not just need rest after travel.

They need decision quality.

That is why bad jet lag hits founders differently than it hits tourists. It does not just make you tired. It makes you slower, fuzzier, and worse at judgment exactly when you still need to operate.

## Why This Matters

Most high-output travelers are not trying to feel perfect.

They are trying to stay useful.

That means the right system is not a wellness routine. It is a performance-preservation system.

## The Founder Travel System

Think in four layers:

1. Protect the first meaningful work block after arrival
2. Protect the first full night of destination sleep
3. Reduce random decisions on the travel day
4. Use a simple rule set instead of complicated optimization

## What This Looks Like

- Know your first priority after landing
- Build your rest and caffeine decisions around that
- Remove optional chaos from arrival day
- Use one calm, pre-decided recovery plan instead of guessing live

## Why This Is Better

People lose more performance to inconsistency than to imperfect plans.

A simple plan you actually follow beats a perfect protocol you abandon halfway through the trip.

## Close

The real edge is not squeezing every variable. It is arriving with enough clarity to still make good decisions.
