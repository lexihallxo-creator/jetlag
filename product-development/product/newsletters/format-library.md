# Format Library

Use these recurring formats to avoid sounding like every other newsletter.

## 1. Arrival Autopsy

Break down why a specific type of traveler lands feeling terrible and what to do differently next time.

Why it works:

- Feels diagnostic and specific
- Creates strong empathy
- Naturally leads into JetLag's planning logic

## 2. Time-Zone Playbook

A tactical issue built around a real scenario, such as `New York to Paris for a Monday morning meeting`.

Why it works:

- Highly practical
- Easy to forward
- Useful even for non-customers

## 3. The JetLag Myth

Take one oversimplified belief and break it apart.

Examples:

- `You just need melatonin`
- `You should force yourself onto local time immediately`
- `Sleep on the plane no matter what`

Why it works:

- Strong hook
- Builds authority
- Feels differentiated

## 4. Traveler Systems

Show a repeatable system used by seasoned travelers to reduce recovery time.

Why it works:

- Turns content into identity
- Appeals to founders, executives, and digital nomads

## 5. The 72-Hour Recovery Blueprint

Give a structured plan for the first 72 hours after landing in a certain scenario.

Why it works:

- Directly useful
- Connects cleanly to the JetLag product

## 6. JetLag Signals

Short issue combining 3-5 observations from real traveler pain points, competitor mentions, and scanner insights.

Why it works:

- Easy to produce consistently
- Keeps market sensing visible
- Feels fresh and current

## Recommended Mix

- 1 deep tactical issue per week
- 1 shorter insight-driven issue per week
- occasional launch or offer issues tied to concierge plans or new product features
