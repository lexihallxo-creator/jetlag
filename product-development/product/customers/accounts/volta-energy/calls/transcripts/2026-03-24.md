## Data Export — The Expansion Unlock (7:30 - 20:00)

**Nina Vasquez:** So here's what I want to spend most of our time on. I showed the energy monitoring dashboard to our VP of Operations last week. His first question was "can I get this as a weekly PDF in my inbox?" When I said no, the excitement just drained from his face. We're so close to cracking the non-technical user base.

**Hannah Stulberg:** Tell me more about that interaction. What was he hoping to do with the PDF?

**Nina Vasquez:** He wanted to send it to facilities managers at our client sites. These are the people who manage the buildings where we install solar panels — they're not engineers, they don't want to log into a dashboard. They want a 3-page PDF that shows this month's energy production, efficiency trends, and any maintenance items. They want to print it and put it in a binder.

**Casey Nguyen:** So the use case is essentially automated reporting from dashboard data?

**Nina Vasquez:** Exactly. And it's not just our VP. The moment we can export dashboard data to PDF, we unlock an entirely new buyer inside our customers. Right now only engineers use Forge. But our energy consultants — the people who actually sell the projects — they need reports they can hand to a facilities manager. Give me PDF export and I can double our seat count by end of quarter.

**Hannah Stulberg:** Walk me through that math. How does PDF export translate to doubled seats?

**Nina Vasquez:** Right now we have 8 engineers on Forge. If I can show the energy consulting team — that's 6 people — that Forge can generate client-facing PDF reports from the same dashboards we build for internal monitoring, they'd want accounts immediately. They currently spend 2 days a month manually compiling those reports in PowerPoint. And then the facilities team — 4 people — they want to build equipment maintenance tracking tools. So that's 8 existing plus 6 consultants plus 4 facilities, at minimum.

**Casey Nguyen:** That's 18 seats from 8. And the consulting team use case is interesting because it's a different buyer persona entirely — these are revenue-generating users, not just internal productivity.

**Nina Vasquez:** Exactly right. The consultants are the revenue engine of our company. If Forge helps them close deals faster by giving them polished client-facing reports, the ROI argument writes itself.

**Hannah Stulberg:** What format would the export need to be? Just PDF, or CSV as well?

**Nina Vasquez:** Both, but PDF is the priority. The facilities managers want formatted, visual reports. CSV is useful for the data team when they want to do further analysis in Excel, but that's a secondary use case.

**Casey Nguyen:** On the technical side — would you need the PDF to be a pixel-perfect replica of the dashboard, or is a reformatted report layout acceptable?

**Casey Nguyen:** That's helpful. A report-oriented PDF template would be more feasible than screenshot-to-PDF, and it sounds like it would actually serve the use case better.

**Hannah Stulberg:** Casey, can you evaluate the technical feasibility on our end? I'm thinking headless rendering versus client-side generation — what's the right approach?

**Casey Nguyen:** I'll look into both. Headless rendering gives us more control over the PDF output and works for server-side scheduled generation, which Nina will want for the weekly email use case. I'll have a feasibility assessment by April 3.
