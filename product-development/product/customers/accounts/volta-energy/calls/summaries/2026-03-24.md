## Company Context

Volta Energy is a renewable energy company with an 8-person engineering team. This is a follow-up from the March 13 feature review. The component decomposition approach that Jordan shared has improved their dashboard workflow significantly. The conversation has shifted from technical pain points to expansion opportunities — two new teams want Forge access, and Nina sees data export as the key to unlocking non-engineering buyers.

## Key Themes

### 1. Dashboard Iteration Progress (Positive)

The component decomposition approach Jordan recommended on March 13 has been working well. Carlos restructured the energy grid monitoring dashboard into 6 isolated component files, and inline editing is no longer breaking the surrounding layout. Nina reported that the team's iteration speed on dashboard changes has roughly doubled since adopting the pattern. They've also deployed a third tool to production — the solar panel performance monitor for their Sunnyvale facility — bringing their deployed count to 3 of 6.

### 2. Data Export to CSV/PDF (Pain Point — P0 for Expansion)

Nina's most urgent request is data export. She sees this as the single feature that would unlock an entirely new category of buyer within Volta's customer base — the non-technical energy consultants and facilities managers who need formatted reports, not interactive dashboards. "The moment we can export dashboard data to PDF, we unlock an entirely new buyer inside our customers. Right now only engineers use Forge. But our energy consultants — the people who actually sell the projects — they need reports they can hand to a facilities manager. Give me PDF export and I can double our seat count by end of quarter." Casey noted that this expansion pattern — from engineering tool to business-wide platform — maps directly to Forge's growth model.

### 3. New Team Expansion Signal (Positive — Expansion)

Two additional teams at Volta — facilities management and field operations — have requested access to Forge. Nina has been evangelizing internally based on the success of the engineering team's dashboards. The facilities team wants equipment maintenance tracking interfaces, and the field ops team wants mobile-friendly site inspection checklists. Nina is confident she can bring both teams on if the deployment experience improves and data export ships. This would roughly triple Volta's seat count from 8 to 20-24 users.
