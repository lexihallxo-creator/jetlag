## Company Context

Pinnacle Media is a media company with a 9-person team running 7 active projects on Forge. Their workflow is design-heavy, originating in Figma before moving to development. Renewal is May 2026, and Leo Martinez (champion) is actively evaluating competitors, making this the most likely account to churn in the next 90 days.

## Key Themes

### 1. Figma-to-Code Gap (Pain Point — P0)

The single biggest risk to this account. Sofia Huang spends her entire workweek in Figma producing pixel-perfect designs, and the handoff to development requires engineers to manually translate every frame. Leo has been evaluating Lovable for the past three weeks specifically because of their design-to-code capabilities, though he was candid that "their generated code is garbage compared to Forge." Sofia framed the gap clearly: "If I could drag a Figma frame into Forge and get code that matches my design, that would eliminate the biggest bottleneck on our team." This is a blocking issue for renewal — without a credible timeline on Figma import, we are likely to lose this account.

### 2. Performance at Scale (Pain Point — P0)

Pinnacle built a content management tool in Forge that works well with small datasets but degrades badly at production scale. Their content catalog has 12,000 items, and the app becomes unusable because generated code defaults to client-side filtering. Leo specifically called out the need for virtual scrolling and server-side filtering. He acknowledged the tool "handles 500 items beautifully" but said the gap between prototype and production performance is a real blocker for expanding Forge usage to more projects.

### 3. Template Flexibility for Media Use Cases (Pain Point — P1)

The template customizer doesn't accommodate media-specific layout patterns. Sofia needs video grids, image galleries with lightbox functionality, and responsive media layouts that aren't available in the current template library. Taylor Brooks took detailed notes and acknowledged this as a gap in our vertical coverage. Sofia was complimentary about Forge's baseline design quality — "the UIs actually look good" — but needs more flexibility for media-specific patterns.

### 4. Generation Quality (Positive)

Despite the frustrations, both Leo and Sofia remain genuinely impressed with the quality of Forge's code output. Leo called it out as the primary reason he wants to stay. Sofia praised the typography hierarchy, responsive breakpoints, and proper spacing in generated UIs, noting that "other AI tools generate things that look like a developer designed them." This is our strongest retention lever — the quality gap between Forge and competitors is real and the customer sees it clearly.
