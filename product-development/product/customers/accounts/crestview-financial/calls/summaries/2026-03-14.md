## Company Context
Crestview Financial is a mid-market financial services firm with a 40-person engineering team using Forge across 18 active projects. All current usage is restricted to non-customer-facing internal tools because SSO and audit log capabilities haven't passed their security review. They onboarded in April 2025 and represent Forge's highest ARR account with significant expansion potential ($300K projected).

## Key Themes
### 1. SSO/SAML Rollout Timeline (Pain Point — P0)
Marcus opened the call by emphasizing urgency around SSO. He has a board presentation in April and needs to report the status of every tool in their stack that lacks SAML/OIDC integration. He was direct: "I'm going to my board in April and they're going to ask me the status of every tool without SSO. If I can't tell them Forge has a ship date for SAML, I'll have to recommend restricting usage. I don't want to do that — my team loves this tool." Riley walked through our current SSO architecture work and confirmed SAML is in active development, but we couldn't commit to a specific GA date on the call. Marcus asked us to follow up with a written timeline by end of March.

### 2. Audit Log Gaps (Pain Point — P0)
Diana walked us through a specific incident from the previous week where she attempted to trace who deployed a financial reporting tool to production. She screen-shared the current audit log view and demonstrated the gap — she could see the project existed but couldn't determine who created, modified, or deployed it. She flagged this as a direct SOC 2 finding: "There's no audit trail. I can see the project exists, but I can't tell you who created it, who modified it, or who deployed it. That's a SOC 2 finding we can't have." Riley acknowledged the gap and noted that deploy-level attribution is on our Q2 roadmap.

### 3. Data Residency & Compliance Roadmap (Pain Point — P1)
Marcus raised data residency toward the end of the call. Some of Crestview's clients have contractual requirements around where data is processed and stored. He asked whether Forge can guarantee US-only data residency today. We confirmed we're US-hosted but don't yet have formal data residency controls or documentation. Marcus said this isn't blocking today but will be a factor in their next compliance review cycle.

### 4. Internal Tool Adoption (Positive)
Despite the security blockers, Marcus was genuinely enthusiastic about the product itself. He shared that his team built a risk assessment calculator in a single afternoon that would have taken two full sprints with their traditional development process. He made clear the issue isn't product quality — it's the enterprise compliance wrapper: "The product is great. It's the enterprise wrapper that needs to catch up."
