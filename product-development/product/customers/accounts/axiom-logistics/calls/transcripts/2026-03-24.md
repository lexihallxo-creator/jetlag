## Preview Environments (10:30 - 20:15)

**Hannah Stulberg:** You mentioned in your email that you wanted to discuss preview environments. Tell us what you're looking for.

**Deepa Patel:** Now that rate limiting is solved, the next thing on my list is preview environments. I want my team to deploy to a staging URL, share it with stakeholders for approval, and then promote to production with one click. Right now they're deploying straight to prod and crossing their fingers.

**Riley Patel:** What does your current review process look like before a production deploy?

**Deepa Patel:** It's informal and that's the problem. An engineer builds something in Forge, shows it to their manager on a screen share, and if the manager says it looks good, they hit deploy. There's no way for a stakeholder — say, a warehouse operations lead who requested the tool — to actually click through it in a real environment before it goes live.

**Riley Patel:** So you want a shareable staging URL that mirrors the production deployment, but is isolated from prod data and accessible to non-technical reviewers?

**Deepa Patel:** Exactly. A URL I can send to a warehouse ops lead and say "click through this, tell me if it works for your team." And then when they approve, I click one button and it's live. No re-deploy, no config changes, just promote.

**Hannah Stulberg:** This is something we've been thinking about. Riley, where are we on preview environments internally?

**Riley Patel:** We have an early design spec. The model we're exploring is per-branch preview deploys — every branch gets an auto-generated preview URL, and merging to main triggers a production deploy. But I'd love to get Deepa's input on whether that model fits their workflow or if they need something different.

**Deepa Patel:** That could work. The key requirements for us are: the preview URL needs to be shareable without requiring a Forge login, it needs to use staging data rather than production data, and the promotion step needs to be explicit — I don't want an automatic deploy to prod on merge without someone clicking a button.

**Hannah Stulberg:** Riley, can you put together a design spec that incorporates Deepa's requirements and share it with her by April 2?

**Riley Patel:** Absolutely. I'll include the branch-based model with an approval gate before production promotion.
