## Company Context

Axiom Logistics is a national logistics and supply chain company with a 28-person engineering team running 16 active projects on Forge. They rely heavily on Forge for production-grade internal tools — particularly real-time logistics dashboards, route optimization UIs, and warehouse management interfaces.

## Key Themes

### 1. API Rate Limiting at Scale (Pain Point — P0)

Axiom's platform team is consistently hitting Forge's API rate limits during peak usage windows. Their logistics dashboard suite makes over 500 API calls per minute between 2-4 PM ET when regional logistics teams across the Eastern seaboard are simultaneously running afternoon route reports and inventory reconciliation. Deepa described it as a daily operational disruption: "We're hitting rate limits every afternoon between 2 and 4 PM when our logistics teams are all running reports simultaneously. That's exactly when we need Forge most, and it's exactly when it falls over." Morgan confirmed the throttling pattern aligns with what the infra team has observed on their end and committed to investigating dedicated capacity tiers for Enterprise accounts.

### 2. CI/CD Integration Gaps (Pain Point — P0)

Raj's DevOps team has blocked the production rollout of 4 new internal tools because Forge lacks native CI/CD pipeline integration. Their entire deployment workflow runs through Jenkins, and the team refuses to introduce a manual deployment step outside that pipeline. Raj was direct: "Our DevOps team refuses to adopt Forge for production deploys until we can plug it into our existing CI/CD pipeline. They don't want another manual step — they want it in the same Jenkins workflow as everything else." This is a concrete blocker to expansion within their organization.

### 3. Generation Quality for Complex Components (Positive)

Deepa praised Forge's generation quality for data-heavy table components. Their warehouse inventory interface — which renders 10,000+ row datasets with inline filtering, column sorting, and conditional formatting — was generated in a single prompt pass with minimal iteration. She called it "the best AI-generated data table we've seen from any tool" and said it saved her team roughly 3 weeks of frontend development time.
