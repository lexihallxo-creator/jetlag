## Company Context
Meridian Health is a healthcare technology company with a 35-person engineering team and 22 active Forge projects — the highest project count across all accounts. They use Forge for both internal tools and patient-facing app prototyping. Their contract is up for renewal in May 2026.

## Key Themes
### 1. Prototype-to-Production Acceleration (Positive)
Anil opened with strong praise for Forge's impact on their development velocity. Their prototype-to-production cycle has compressed from three weeks to three days — a transformation he called "not incremental." He credited Forge with enabling his team to rapidly iterate on clinical workflow tools, patient intake prototypes, and internal dashboards. This speed is a core part of their renewal justification.

### 2. HIPAA Audit Trail Requirements (Pain Point — P0)
Dr. Pham raised critical concerns about audit trail capabilities for any application that touches protected health information. She needs to produce reports showing every user who accessed a patient-facing prototype, what data it queried, and when — a hard requirement for HIPAA compliance. The current audit logs don't capture data access patterns at this granularity. She was unambiguous: "If I can't produce that report during an audit, we can't use Forge for anything that touches PHI. Period." This directly limits how much of their work can move onto Forge and is a factor in the renewal conversation.

### 3. Webhook Reliability for EHR Integration (Pain Point — P0)
Anil described three instances in the past month where webhooks from Forge-deployed applications to their EHR system were dropped. Each dropped webhook resulted in a missed notification to a care coordinator — a patient safety concern in their context. He was emphatic that healthcare notifications require guaranteed delivery: "A missed notification isn't an inconvenience — it's a patient safety issue." He requested a retry mechanism with exponential backoff and a dead letter queue for failed deliveries. Morgan acknowledged the severity and discussed potential architectural approaches.

### 4. BAA and Environment Isolation (Pain Point — P1)
Dr. Pham asked whether Forge offers a Business Associate Agreement for healthcare customers — a standard requirement for any vendor handling PHI. We confirmed we don't currently have a BAA in place. She also requested environment isolation so that applications touching PHI can be logically separated from non-sensitive projects, with different access controls and audit requirements for each environment tier.
