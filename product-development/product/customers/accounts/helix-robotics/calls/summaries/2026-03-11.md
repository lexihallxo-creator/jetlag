## Company Context

Helix Robotics is a robotics company with a 15-person engineering team and 11 active Forge projects. They use Forge primarily to prototype web-based control panels, dashboards, and monitoring interfaces for their hardware products. Their engineers are distributed across Austin, Munich, and Shenzhen.

## Key Themes

### 1. Real-Time Collaboration (Pain Point — P0)

Helix's hardware design review process is fundamentally bottlenecked by the lack of real-time collaboration in Forge. Rachel described their current review meetings as "chaos" — engineers across three time zones join a call, take turns screen-sharing their prototype edits, and iterate sequentially. A review session that should take 30 minutes stretches to 90+ because only one person can drive at a time. Rachel stressed that async commenting is not a viable workaround; their review cadence requires synchronous, multi-user editing with live cursor visibility. "Our hardware review meetings are chaos," Rachel said. "I need live cursors, live editing, everyone in the same canvas at the same time."

### 2. Serialized Editing Workflow (Pain Point — P1)

Ben expanded on the collaboration pain by describing how his workflow with Rachel is completely serialized. When prototyping a hardware control interface, Ben handles the control panel components while Rachel focuses on the data visualization layer. Today, they have to wait for each other to finish before reviewing or iterating. Ben estimated this serialization doubles their review cycle time and creates unnecessary context-switching overhead. He wants the ability to work on separate components of the same project simultaneously.

### 3. Python/FastAPI Support (Feature Request — P1)

Rachel raised interest in Python and FastAPI framework support for firmware-adjacent prototyping. Several of Helix's backend services are written in Python, and the ability to prototype lightweight FastAPI endpoints alongside their React frontends would let them validate end-to-end flows more quickly. This is not blocking current usage but would expand their Forge footprint significantly.
