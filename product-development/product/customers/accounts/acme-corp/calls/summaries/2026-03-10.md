# Company Context

Acme Corp is a 50-person B2B SaaS startup based in Austin, TX, building workforce management software. They adopted Forge 6 months ago to accelerate internal tool development. Their engineering team of 12 uses Forge primarily for rapid prototyping of admin dashboards, data visualization tools, and customer-facing onboarding flows.

Current usage:
- 8 active Forge projects
- 12 team seats
- Averaging 35 generation runs per week
- Deployed 3 production apps built with Forge in the last quarter

# Key Themes

## 1. Generation Quality (Positive)

Acme's team is consistently impressed with the quality of generated code. They highlighted that Forge's output requires minimal manual cleanup compared to competitors they evaluated (Lovable, Bolt). Derek specifically called out the React component architecture as "production-grade out of the box."

## 2. Deployment Friction (Pain Point - High Priority)

The biggest pain point raised was the deployment workflow. Today, Acme exports generated code from Forge, manually configures AWS infrastructure, and deploys through their own CI/CD pipeline. This adds 2-3 days to what should be a same-day workflow. They want native deployment support, ideally one-click deploy to AWS with configurable options for VPC, region, and environment variables.

## 3. Team Collaboration (Pain Point - Medium Priority)

With 12 engineers now using Forge, they're running into collaboration gaps. Specific issues:
- No way to share project templates across the team
- No visibility into what others are building
- Version history is per-user, not shared
- Comments and review workflows don't exist

## 4. Enterprise Readiness (Forward-Looking)

Tom (CTO) mentioned they're evaluating SOC 2 compliance requirements and will need SSO integration within the next 6 months. He asked about our enterprise roadmap timeline.
