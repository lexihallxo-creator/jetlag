## Company Context

Acme Corp is a 50-person B2B SaaS startup with 12 engineers using Forge across 8 active projects, averaging 35 generation runs per week. They adopted Forge 6 months ago for internal tool development and have deployed 5 production apps to date. Champion is Derek Liu (VP Engineering).

## Key Themes

### 1. Version Rollback Reliability (Pain Point — P0)

Acme experienced a production incident on March 13 where an engineer attempted to roll back a project to a previous version, and the rollback silently dropped three API endpoints from the generated output. The team did not discover the missing endpoints until 40 minutes into the outage, when downstream services began failing. Derek described this as a trust-breaking moment — if rollback is unreliable, they cannot use Forge for production workloads with confidence. He was visibly frustrated and emphasized that version history "can't just be a nice-to-have — it needs to be rock solid." Jordan acknowledged the severity and committed to investigating the root cause.

### 2. Version Diff Visibility (Pain Point — P1)

Priya flagged that the current version history UI shows a list of timestamps but provides no visibility into what actually changed between versions. Without a diff view, engineers are "rolling back blind" — they cannot assess the impact of a rollback before executing it. Priya requested a visual diff similar to GitHub's PR diff view, showing added, removed, and modified lines across all files in the project. She noted that a proper diff view would have prevented the March 13 incident, since the engineer would have seen the missing API endpoints before confirming the rollback.

### 3. One-Click Deploy Beta Progress (Positive — P1)

On a positive note, Acme has been actively using the One-Click Deploy beta and is very satisfied with the experience. They deployed two additional apps this month — an internal reporting dashboard and a customer feedback form — and Derek described the process as taking "minutes instead of days." This is a significant improvement over their previous workflow of manually configuring AWS infrastructure. Derek said the deploy improvements are "the kind of thing that gets the whole team excited" and credited it with increasing overall Forge enthusiasm across the engineering team.
