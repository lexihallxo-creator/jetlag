# JetLag Jobs To Be Done And Users

## Primary Users

- Frequent business travelers who need to function quickly after long-haul flights
- Leisure travelers with high-stakes trips who want recovery help without researching protocols
- Digital nomads and frequent international travelers optimizing sleep, energy, and performance

## Core Jobs To Be Done

- Help me prepare for a major time-zone shift without doing my own research.
- Turn my itinerary into a personalized recovery schedule I can trust.
- Remind me what to do at the right moment while I am traveling.
- Help me recover faster so I can perform, explore, or rest sooner after arrival.

## Main Pain Points

- Most jet lag advice is generic and not specific to the actual trip.
- Travelers do not want to manually calculate time-zone adjustments.
- Complicated schedules are hard to follow during real travel disruptions.
- Users need help in the moment, not just static educational content.

## Product Implications

- Onboarding must create value quickly from minimal trip input.
- Recommendations need to feel practical, calm, and realistic.
- Notification timing is a core product experience, not a side feature.
- The product should reinforce trust with simple reasoning and credible science.
