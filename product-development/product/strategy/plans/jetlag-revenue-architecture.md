# jetlag revenue architecture

## objective

map the highest-margin, non-ecommerce revenue model for JetLag so product and go-to-market decisions support the right business shape.

## strategic insight

ecommerce can help with entry and conversion, but the real business should be built on systems, authority, and premium access.

## revenue layers

### layer 1: consumer and concierge entry point

purpose:

- demand capture
- trust building
- fast cash flow
- feedback collection

offers:

- jetlag express concierge plan
- newsletter
- future lightweight subscription

### layer 2: premium high-touch revenue

purpose:

- higher-margin monetization
- better customer understanding
- brand signal

offers:

- executive concierge
- founder travel support
- premium arrival planning

### layer 3: enterprise programs

purpose:

- largest near-to-midterm revenue opportunity
- recurring contracts
- stronger strategic moat

offers:

- travel performance workshops
- leadership travel protocols
- team dashboards
- executive readiness programs

### layer 4: licensing and ecosystem

purpose:

- scalable distribution
- brand expansion
- b2b2c monetization

offers:

- licensed protocols
- partner integrations
- travel performance modules for airlines, hotels, and lounges

### layer 5: certification and insight products

purpose:

- ecosystem growth
- margin-rich digital revenue
- authority building

offers:

- certification
- training
- performance insight products
- aggregated data products later

## priority order

1. concierge proof and outcomes
2. premium executive offer
3. enterprise travel performance offer
4. licensing conversations
5. certification and data products

## implications for product

the product should not be designed only for casual consumer use.

it should create:

- measurable outcomes
- strong positioning
- reusable protocols
- premium trust
- reporting potential for enterprise buyers
