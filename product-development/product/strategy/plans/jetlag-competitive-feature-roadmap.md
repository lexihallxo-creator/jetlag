# jetlag competitive feature roadmap

## objective

turn competitive research into a practical sequence of product work that gives jetlag a real wedge instead of becoming a weaker copy of existing jet lag apps.

## competitive reality

### timeshifter is strongest at

- trust and scientific authority
- personalization around biological time
- practicality for real travel conditions
- timed notifications
- pre-travel adjustment
- short-trip support
- complex itinerary handling
- flight editing when plans change

### stopjetlag is strongest at

- comprehensive timing guidance
- exact recommendations across bright light, sleep, meals, caffeine, activity, and optional melatonin
- handling connecting flights
- dense execution detail for people who want a full protocol

### newer trip-oriented apps are strongest at

- combining jet lag planning with broader trip context
- itinerary and activity planning
- offline usage and travel utility beyond recovery alone

## what jetlag should not do

- do not try to win first on generic “science-backed jet lag advice”
- do not ship a static plan generator and call it done
- do not position the product as only a circadian calculator

## best differentiation angle

jetlag should own:

`travel performance for the first 48 hours after arrival`

that means the product should optimize for:

- first meeting readiness
- first work block clarity
- first day energy
- first night recovery
- realistic followability during messy travel

## product principles from the gap analysis

- optimize for the first important moment after landing
- adapt to real-world travel changes
- prioritize followable actions over dense protocols
- make the user feel more prepared, not more overloaded
- build trust through clarity, proof, and post-trip learning

## roadmap

### phase 1: must-have parity plus better framing

#### 1. adaptive arrival-first plan engine

build a plan engine that uses:

- route
- departure time
- arrival time
- number of time zones crossed
- normal sleep schedule
- first important event after arrival

why:

- this gives jetlag a differentiated planning input that competitors do not foreground as clearly

success signal:

- users say the plan feels built for their real trip, not a generic time-zone shift

#### 2. timed action system

support timed actions for:

- bright light
- light avoidance
- caffeine timing
- naps
- sleep target
- optional supplements later, if appropriate

why:

- strong competitors are not winning with information alone, but with timing and execution

success signal:

- first-day action completion rate becomes measurable and useful

#### 3. trip editing and instant regeneration

allow users to:

- edit flights
- account for delays
- update arrival windows
- regenerate the plan quickly

why:

- travel changes constantly and static plans break trust fast

success signal:

- users do not abandon the plan after trip disruption

### phase 2: wedge expansion

#### 4. trip-type presets

create presets for:

- business trip
- founder trip
- vacation trip
- athlete or performance trip
- short turnaround
- family trip

why:

- this creates clearer positioning, better onboarding, and easier conversion paths

success signal:

- stronger activation and better website message-match

#### 5. practicality mode

offer plan styles such as:

- aggressive
- balanced
- easiest to follow

why:

- timeshifter already leans into practicality. jetlag needs its own explicit answer to real-life friction.

success signal:

- fewer users ignore the plan because it feels unrealistic

#### 6. first-48-hours view

make the first 48 hours after landing the core surface:

- arrival window
- first night
- second day
- key do / avoid list

why:

- this is the strongest product framing opportunity for jetlag

success signal:

- the product becomes easier to explain and more memorable

### phase 3: trust and retention

#### 7. post-trip feedback loop

after each trip, ask:

- how severe was your jet lag
- what actions did you follow
- what failed
- what did you wish the plan handled better

why:

- this improves the product and builds proof over time

success signal:

- you accumulate testimonials, product insight, and real outcome data

#### 8. progress and results memory

store:

- past trips
- plan versions
- user-reported outcomes
- preferred plan style

why:

- repeated travelers should feel the product getting smarter each trip

success signal:

- second-trip retention improves

### phase 4: strategic expansion

#### 9. richer itinerary awareness

support:

- stopovers
- multi-city trips
- return legs
- short business turnarounds

why:

- this is where higher-intent travelers start to see JetLag as serious

success signal:

- better fit for frequent travelers and premium users

#### 10. travel-performance ecosystem

later, expand into:

- first-day scheduling guidance
- travel-day energy protection
- activity or work-readiness planning

why:

- this moves JetLag from “jet lag app” to “travel performance system”

success signal:

- stronger long-term brand moat

## what to build first

if resources are tight, build in this exact order:

1. adaptive arrival-first plan engine
2. timed action system
3. trip editing and instant regeneration
4. trip-type presets
5. first-48-hours view
6. post-trip feedback loop

## what to measure

- signup to first plan completion
- first-day action completion
- flight-edit regeneration usage
- second-trip retention
- post-trip reported usefulness
- conversion by trip type

## why this roadmap is better than a generic roadmap

it does not try to out-timeshifter on pure authority first.

it gives jetlag a more ownable product story:

- practical
- arrival-first
- performance-oriented
- adaptable to real travel chaos
