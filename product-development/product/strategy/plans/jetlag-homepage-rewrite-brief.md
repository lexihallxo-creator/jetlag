# jetlag homepage rewrite brief

## objective

rewrite the homepage so it feels like a category-defining travel-performance product, not an early placeholder or generic jet lag planner.

## current problem

the current site explains the offer, but it still feels too much like:

- a launch artifact
- a concierge page
- a placeholder for a future app

it does not yet create a strong enough impression that jetlag is different from the existing category.

## positioning shift

move from:

`personalized jet lag recovery plan`

to:

`the travel performance system for your first 48 hours after landing`

## homepage message hierarchy

### hero

say:

- what jetlag is
- who it is for
- why it matters immediately

example direction:

`land clear, not wrecked`
`jetlag helps travelers protect the first 48 hours after crossing time zones`

### proof block

the page needs more trust.

add:

- science or method explanation
- concierge results once available
- traveler quotes once available
- language that feels certain and useful, not vague

### differentiation block

show why jetlag is not just another jet lag tool.

compare around:

- arrival-first planning
- travel-performance framing
- realistic execution
- feedback-driven personalization

### how it works

make the logic simple:

1. add the trip
2. set your real travel goal
3. get a timed first-48-hours plan
4. adapt if travel changes

### offer block

keep the concierge offer visible, but make it feel like:

- the first product experience
- not the final product identity

### newsletter block

position the newsletter as:

`travel recovery intelligence`

not:

`updates from us`

### feedback block

invite travelers into the product:

- tell us what ruins your trips
- tell us what current tools miss
- help shape jetlag

## copy principles

- less placeholder energy
- less “coming soon” feel
- more conviction
- more specificity
- more premium calm
- more ownable language

## phrases worth leaning into

- first 48 hours
- arrival window
- travel performance
- first important moment after landing
- built for real trips, not ideal schedules
- clear, timed, practical

## phrases to avoid leaning on too heavily

- generic wellness
- sleep optimization fluff
- abstract productivity language
- broad “ai travel assistant” language

## visual direction

keep:

- beige and white palette
- layered 3d cards
- premium editorial feel
- lowercase typography if that remains part of brand direction

improve:

- stronger hierarchy
- less placeholder emphasis
- more proof and product depth
- clearer conversion structure

## ideal homepage sections

1. hero
2. why travelers lose the first 48 hours
3. what jetlag does differently
4. how the product works
5. concierge offer
6. newsletter
7. feedback
8. final cta

## strongest homepage wedge

if one line has to carry the product, use this direction:

`jetlag helps you protect the first 48 hours after landing`

that is more ownable than generic “beat jet lag” language and more directly tied to real outcomes.
